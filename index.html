Your assignment

You have just been named Crisis Response Lead at Meridian Foods, a mid-size packaged-food company. Ninety minutes ago, three hospitals reported patients with the same illness — and early signals point to one of your products. The information is incomplete, the clock is running, and everyone is watching how you decide.

How it works

  • You will lead through 6 rounds. Each round gives you a briefing (some of it unreliable) and a set of decisions.
  • For every decision you will record your choice, your confidence, the emotion driving you, and a one-line rationale. This becomes your Decision Log.
  • Five forces shift with your choices — and with events you don't control. Good decisions can still get unlucky outcomes. That gap is the point.
  • At the end you'll get a scored report and a downloadable, integrity-stamped log to submit and write about.
Course context — this is your Week 3 simulation activity. You will play three times, changing one or two of your three settings (mindset / decision rule / stakeholder) between each run, then write the Week 4 Decision Analysis paper comparing how your different approaches drove different choices — and outcomes. Play each run in character.
